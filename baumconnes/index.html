Swiss Doctoral Program in Mathematics

Séminaire Kervaire 2021: Baum-Connes Conjecture

24 - 29 Octobre 2021
Les Diablerets, Switzerland

The Baum-Connes conjecture bridges Operator Algebras, Index Theory, Geometric Group Theory and Metric Geometry. It is therefore a unifying theme in Mathematics. The mini-courses will serve as an introduction to the subject, while the talks by younger experts will explain the search towards positive results and also towards counterexamples.

Minicourses (3h each)

  • Pierre Julg, (Orléans):
    1. K-theory, a receptacle for indices
    2. Kasparov theory, a tool for computing K-theory
    3. BC conjecture, some results
  • Alain Valette, (Neuchâtel):
    1. Proper actions and the classifying space for proper actions
    2. The left-hand side: equivariant K-homology
    3. Introduction to the coarse BC conjecture

Lectures (2h each)

  • Sara Azzali (Greifswald): KK-theory with real coefficients and localised Baum-Connes conjecture
  • Indira Chatterji (Nice): Introduction to the Rapid Decay property for discrete groups
  • Ramón Flores (Sevilla): Computing the left-hand-side. Slides
  • Maria Paula Gomez Aparicio (Paris-Saclay): Lafforgue's approach and the Oka principle
  • Omar Mohsen (Paris-Saclay): The Higson-Kasparov theorem
  • Shintaro Nishikawa (Muenster): On localization algebras and the gamma element method
  • Hervé Oyono-Oyono (Metz): Controlled K-theory. Slides1, Slides2
  • Mikael de la Salle (ENS Lyon): Strong property (T)
  • Damian Sawicki (KU Leuven): Warped cones violating the coarse Baum-Connes conjecture
